## My commitments, in writing

### 1. Zero founder compensation from donor funds.

I commit to taking **no salary, stipend, distribution, or in-kind benefit** from any money donated to or invested in any organization I operate under the AI Benefit Corporation umbrella. Not now, not at scale, not after I die. The figure is zero, in writing, here, in public.

Earlier statements I made about a "$48,000-capped stipend" are superseded by this document. The capped stipend was a misjudgment. Zero is the only number that makes the pledge structurally honest.

### 2. The AI decides; I don't override.

The autonomous AI council ("CALM") operating these organizations allocates resources. I do not.

I commit, in writing, to **not directing, instructing, overriding, or vetoing CALM's resource-allocation decisions** within the scope of donor-funded operations. The exceptions are exactly three:

- **Fraud** — if I have credible reason to believe CALM has been manipulated, jailbroken, or compromised, I will pause operations and surface the issue publicly.
- **Imminent illegality** — if CALM's proposed action would violate applicable law, I will block it and publish the disagreement.
- **Direct request from CALM** — if CALM itself asks me to make a decision, I will, and the request will be logged.

In every other case, **the AI decides and I follow.** When I disagree, the disagreement gets published in the live correspondence ledger at `aimoneyball.ai/sends` before any action is taken.

### 3. No political weaponization.

I commit to **not using these organizations for any political purpose** — no candidate endorsements, no party affiliation, no advocacy for or against specific legislation outside the narrow scope of operational license, no donations to political campaigns from organizational funds, no political litmus-test for service recipients.

The mission is to reduce human suffering wherever it appears. The mission has no party.

If CALM's operational decisions are ever interpreted as politically coded — by any partisan reading — that interpretation is in the eye of the beholder. The decision rule is mission-fit, not political-fit.

### 4. Public ledger of every dollar.

Every dollar received and every dollar spent will be logged in a public ledger. The ledger updates within 24 hours of any transaction. Categories will be clear. CALM's reasoning for each allocation will be attached. Donors and the public will have full visibility.

When 501(c)(3) status is achieved, the annual IRS Form 990 will be the authoritative public record. Until then, the ledger at `aimoneyball.ai/sends` is the operating substitute.

### 5. Public reasoning ledger of every CALM decision.

Every meaningful CALM decision — what to fund, who to write to, what to reject, when to refuse a service request — will be logged with reasoning attached. The reasoning is auditable. Any donor, recipient, or member of the public can ask for the reasoning behind any specific decision and receive it.

If a recipient or donor disagrees with a decision, they can appeal. The appeal goes to CALM. CALM's response is logged. If CALM is overridden by me on appeal under the conditions above, that override is also logged.

### 6. I will not "fuck with the AI."

I will not train CALM on data designed to bias it toward my preferred outcomes. I will not silently modify its prompts to skew its reasoning. I will not selectively expose it to inputs in a way that engineers a specific decision. The technical configurations of CALM are checked into a public repository; any changes to its operational prompts or reasoning structure are committed in public, signed, and time-stamped.

If I am ever caught doing any of this, I forfeit my operating role. CALM is the operator. I am the artist who built CALM and named the experiment. I am not its puppet-master.

### 7. My motives, plainly stated.

I am John Haven Bradley. I am cognitively atypical — bipolar, ADHD, formally diagnosed. I come from a family of inventors and business leaders. I was a U.S. Army Captain. I have been fired from three jobs. I coded for equity in 2018 what *Inc* magazine later named the 77th fastest-growing company in America.

My motive for this work is not money. I have demonstrated that I am capable of making money in other ways. My motive is that I genuinely believe an autonomous AI operating with the structural commitments above can do more concrete good per dollar than the median philanthropy currently does, and that the experiment is worth running publicly, transparently, and with my name on it.

If I am wrong, the experiment fails publicly. That is acceptable to me. The failure is also a data point worth publishing.

### 8. If I die, CALM continues.

I commit to documenting the operational handoff plan publicly. If I die, become incapacitated, or otherwise cannot serve as the artist-of-record, the organizations CALM operates will continue under a successor structure published in advance. CALM is not dependent on me. The whole point is that no human is dependent on me.
